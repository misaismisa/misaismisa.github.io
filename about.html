HI! I'M MISA NGUYEN.

A UX/UI Designer with a passion for seeing and interacting with the cultures of the world.

01 //

MY FORAY INTO THE WORLD OF ART AND DESIGN

At the age of 3 I wanted to be the next Monet. At the age of 10, I began to interact with computers and learned what computer graphics were. I found myself heavily invested in learning the basics of graphic design using Photoshop and blog layout design using html and css. I joined forums where I could make nifty little animated gifs before memes were popular and would spend countless hours on dafont.com staring at all the fonts they had to offer. Realizing now, how typography has an impact on the way we view the products that we interact with on an everyday basis.

02 //

DEVIATION AND EXPERIENCES ABROAD

After studying politics and human behavior in an international setting, my dream of living abroad came true when I moved to Tokyo, Japan for 3 years. I had the opportunity to teach English in a corporate setting and worked in the pharmaceutical recruitment industry thereafter. Working in a fast-paced, hard-hitting environment where I was constantly assessing business KPIs and speaking with top professionals on a daily basis, gave me the skillset to negotiate and work with clientele under pressure.

03 //

FINDING UX AND PURSUING THE DREAM

Exploring a career in fashion back in California after leaving Japan and backpacking solo for 3 to 4 months in Southeast Asia, reignited my interests in design and the arts. Working as the Operations Manager for the major fashion house, Chanel, I learned to appreciate the intricate detailing that went into embroidery and designs that were hand stitched for the brand’s bags. I grew increasingly curious about how both good and bad design affects how the user interacts with a technological interface and I chose Bloc as my apprenticeship program to guide me in learning the hard skills. Knowing now that design comes hand in hand with the business needs of a company, my confidence to interact client side with my detailed eye for design is my greatest asset. Design goes beyond just looking like another pretty interface but is layers of research and thought, centered around human behavior.
